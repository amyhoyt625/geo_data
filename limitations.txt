LIMITATIONS OF THE STUDY 
Our study has certain limitations. The scope of this study is limited to two primary conferences, ICSA and ICSE. While these are two major venues in software engineering, they might not be fully representative of the entire industry. Researchers who primarily post in regional venues may not be captured in our data, potentially understating participation from regions where conference publishing is less common.

The geographic unit of analysis in this study is the country of the author's institutional affiliation at the time of publication, not the author's nationality or country of origin. This study doesn’t take into account the actual geographical origins of the authors. This distinction is important: a researcher from China working at a German institution would be counted as a German-affiliated author. This distinction is particularly relevant for countries with large international research communities, such as the United States, where a significant proportion of researchers at domestic institutions are foreign-born or internationally trained. As a result, participation from such countries may be overstated relative to their domestic research output, while participation from countries with high rates of researcher emigration may be understated. 

There are also some constraints on the coverage of our data. Although most data were able to be matched up between OpenAlex and the DBLP database, with rates of 94.1% for ICSA and 95.9% for ICSE, the remaining roughly five percent or so that were unable to be matched up were excluded from the results.
